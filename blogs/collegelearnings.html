College Learnings

Published on December 13, 2023 by Matthew Leong

I would describe my college life as cut short. With my first two years of college being online, it did not really feel like I was experiencing the most out of it. However, I was still able to learn a lot of things and meet new people. For that, I am thankful.

I am Matthew, currently a 4th Year BS - Information System student in De La Salle - College of Saint Benilde. I am currently taking on my last few terms as a student and will be graduating soon. My collective experience and learnings during college will be what will keep me equipped for the professional world.

Throughout my stay in DLS-CSB, I was able to learn and develop my skills and knowledge. From the basics and fundamentals of programming to the advanced intricacies and deployment of websites, it really has been a long way from where I began. I was able to learn how to develop interactive websites that people could use as well as develop mobile applications that people can install on their phones. I was able to learn programming languages such as Java, C#, and Kotlin.

For my OJT or internship, I was able to get an internship at a startup company named Wizy as a Quality Assurance Associate intern. I was able to apply my learnings from a subject in our curriculum named SOFTEST wherein we got to learn the fundamentals of software testing and different methods to test software or websites.

Overall, college was an experience. The face-to-face experience may have been short, but I was able to make the most of it. Thank you DLS-CSB, thank you to my peers, thank you to my professors, and thank you to myself for surviving college.
